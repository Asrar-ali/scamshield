# Problem Statement

## The gap

Phone scams are the most devastating fraud channel for elderly and vulnerable people, and every existing defense operates **after the money is gone**:

- **Caller-ID blocklists** catch known numbers — scammers rotate and spoof numbers trivially.
- **Bank fraud detection** catches transactions — after the victim has been manipulated into authorizing them, and authorized-push-payment fraud is often not reimbursed.
- **Awareness campaigns** assume the victim recognizes the scam under pressure — the entire scam design exists to prevent exactly that.

The structural gap: scams succeed through **psychological manipulation in real time** — urgency, authority impersonation, isolation ("don't tell your family"), payment redirection — and nothing on the call listens for those tactics *while the call is happening*.

## Why now

Generative voice AI cuts both ways: scammers already clone grandchildren's voices (FBI-tracked, >$5M in 2025). The same technology finally makes the defense possible — an agent fast enough to understand a conversation *as it happens* and name the manipulation before the victim acts. O2 (UK) validated the adjacent concept with "Daisy," an AI grandmother deployed to waste scammers' time; ScamShield goes the next step: from wasting scammers' time to **protecting real victims mid-call**.

## Who it's for

- **Seniors and their families** (consumer): a call-screening bodyguard on grandma's line, opt-in, with family alerts.
- **Telecoms & banks** (B2B): tactic-level risk signal on flagged lines — far richer than number reputation.
